FREESTYLE · CONTENT ENGINE · 13 WEEKS

Show your work.
Every single week.

The floating-pill post proved the thesis: Freestyle's content edge is teardowns and receipts — reverse-engineering the incumbent and publishing proof of every claim. Nobody else in the OSS dictation field does this. The calendar below runs that play aggressively: one blog post, one demo clip, and 2–3 social posts per week, every piece feeding the next.

The rules
BLOG / WRITTEN CLIP / DEMO SOCIAL / X + LINKEDIN DISTRIBUTION EVENT

Every clip is under 30 seconds, screen-recorded, no talking head, text overlay only. Every blog post ends with the repo link and one ask. Every X post has an artifact — a clip, a chart, a diagram, never just words. Blog posts go up Tuesday; clips ride Wednesday/Thursday; build-in-public numbers go out Friday. If a week collapses: ship the clip, skip the post. Clips compound on X; posts can slide a week.

Social formats (repeatable)

  • Friday numbers — stars/downloads/contributors/releases, every week, no exceptions. The streak is the brand.
  • Excalidraw diagram drops — one architecture diagram per week; your native format, infinitely shareable.
  • Contributor spotlights — one per merged external PR.
  • Star-milestone posts — 500, 1K, 2.5K; each with a thank-you and the milestone-feature progress bar.
  • "What should gate the next beta?" polls — engagement that doubles as roadmap research.
  • Measured quote-takes on Wispr/voice-AI news — be the informed commentator, never the bitter competitor. When the privacy story resurfaces, you want to already be the credible voice in the room.
  • Hot-take singles: "Voice dictation is a commodity. $12/mo is a UI tax." / "Your dictation app should fear Wireshark." / "If you can't read the code that hears your voice, it's not your tool."
  • Before/after WPM testimonials — retweet users' own numbers as they post them.
